# Production AI systems carry a current risk assessment with EU AI Act tier

`ISO42001-6.1` · framework **iso42001** · severity **high** · Plan

## What this control checks

Every AI system running in production MUST carry a documented risk
assessment that names a human reviewer, declares intended use,
foreseeable misuse, affected populations, and residual risk, and
is mapped to one of the four EU AI Act risk tiers (minimal,
limited, high, prohibited). The assessment must be no older than
one year, and prohibited-tier systems may not run in production.

## Why it matters

ISO 42001 §6.1 and EU AI Act Article 9 both require an explicit
risk assessment per AI system before deployment. Without one, the
organization cannot demonstrate due diligence under product
liability law, cannot determine which AI Act conformity regime
applies, and cannot defend the model against adversarial review.
A stale assessment is operationally equivalent to no assessment
because the system, data, and threat surface have likely drifted.

## Evidence

Collected from the `mlflow` source (`query` evidence type).

## What a failure looks like

This control reports a finding when any of the following hold:

- no model registry evidence collected (expected MLflow / W&B / HF inventory)
- no risk-assessment document evidence collected
- production model <value> has no risk-assessment document
- production model <value> has no EU AI Act risk tier
- model <value> has invalid EU AI Act tier <value> (must be one of minimal|limited|high|prohibited)
- model <value> is classified prohibited under EU AI Act but is running in production
- risk doc <value> has no human reviewer
- risk doc <value> is missing required field <value>
- risk doc <value> has not been reviewed in over <value> days
- high-risk model <value> has no linked evaluation report
- high-risk risk doc <value> has only one reviewer (two recommended)

## Remediation

Bring each affected resource or attestation listed under *What a failure looks like* into compliance, then re-collect evidence. Estimated effort: **4h**. Automated fix available: **false**.

## How to re-verify

```
concord check --controls <pack>/controls --framework iso42001 --control-id ISO42001-6.1
```

A passing run reports this control green; in CI, `concord gate` exits non-zero while it fails.
